# Proof: Square Root ($\sqrt{x}$)

Construct a circle whose diameter is $x+1$. The perpendicular distance from the join point (where $1$ meets $x$) to the circle's boundary is exactly $\sqrt{x}$.

## Construction
1. Align Segment(1) and Segment($x$) end-to-end.
2. Draw a circle that snaps to both outer ends (Diameter = $x+1$).
3. Draw a vertical line where the segments meet.
4. The intersection between this line and the circle is $\sqrt{x}$.

## Mathematical Basis
This follows from the **Geometric Mean Theorem**. In a circle with diameter $a+b$, the altitude to the diameter is $\sqrt{ab}$. With $a=1$, the altitude is $\sqrt{x}$.

# Proof: Rational Numbers (Q)

Any rational number $m/n$ is constructible if $m$ and $n$ are constructible.

Since integers are definitively constructible by adjoining unit squares end-to-end, constructing $m/n$ simply reduces to division: determining a length $\ell$ such that $n \cdot \ell = m$.

1. This is completed using **Thales's Intercept Theorem** (Similar Triangles).
2. Draw an intersecting angle.
3. Along one ray, mark out lengths $n$ and $m$.
4. Drop parallel lines intersecting the other ray.
5. The projected intersection point yields the exact ratio $m/n$.
